# Phase 8: Launch Polish and Public Launch Design

## Intent

Phase 8 is the final public-launch layer for Veltrix.

The goal is no longer to add another major system. The goal is to make the entire product feel launch-worthy, coherent, premium, and publicly defensible across:

- the public launch site
- the admin portal
- the member-facing webapp
- bot surfaces
- legal and support rails

This phase should make Veltrix feel like one product with one voice and one clear launch posture, not a collection of powerful subsystems that happened to ship in the right order.

## Launch posture

This is a real public launch.

That means Phase 8 must support:

- public-facing acquisition
- self-serve entry
- high-touch entry for serious buyers
- legal and support legitimacy
- final cross-surface quality

Veltrix should launch as a premium hybrid of:

- enterprise trust
- crypto-native momentum

The primary buyer is a mix of founders, growth leads, community operators, and crypto ops teams, with founders as the primary entrypoint.

The CTA posture is dual-track:

- `Start now`
- `Book demo`
- with room for a lower-friction support contact path like `Talk to us`

The public launch cannot rely on fake or thin social proof. There are no customer logos, testimonials, or case studies yet, so the product must sell itself through:

- workflow proof
- product architecture proof
- operator-grade clarity
- visual confidence

## Positioning

Veltrix should not present itself as:

- just a quest tool
- just a community bot
- just a dashboard
- just an analytics product

Veltrix should present itself as:

**the operating system for launches, community execution, member journeys, and safety rails**

That positioning needs to stay consistent across:

- public marketing
- portal copy
- webapp copy
- bot responses
- support and legal framing

## Visual direction

The design direction is an adaptive hybrid:

- the public site should feel lighter, more open, and more editorial
- the product surfaces should remain darker, denser, and more operator-grade
- both still need to feel like the same brand system

Rules for the brand layer:

- no generic SaaS landing page structure
- no fake enterprise blandness
- no noisy crypto-neon chaos
- no card-spam for the sake of modernity

Instead, Veltrix should feel:

- intentional
- premium
- controlled
- technical without feeling sterile
- crypto-native without feeling unserious

## Surface map

Phase 8 covers the full public-launch surface set.

### 1. Public launch site

The public site becomes the main public entrypoint for Veltrix.

Its job is to help a founder or operator immediately understand:

- what Veltrix is
- why it is different from isolated quest or community tools
- how the system fits together
- what their next step should be

Required sections:

- hero
- founder-first product narrative
- system architecture and workflow proof
- product-surface overview
- CTA rail for self-serve and demo
- FAQ
- support and legal footer

The site should emphasize product proof over social proof.

### 2. Admin portal

The admin portal already has the right operational depth. Phase 8 is about final product-grade polish.

It must feel:

- intentional
- consistent
- easy to scan
- free of hidden expert-only flows

Launch-complete portal quality means:

- clearer hierarchy
- cleaner action placement
- consistent empty states
- stronger helper copy
- consistent responsive behavior
- no "magic routes" needed to find core tools

The most important portal surfaces for final polish are:

- Overview
- Analytics
- Claims
- Moderation
- On-chain
- Community
- Campaign Studio
- Quest Studio
- Raid Studio
- Launch Workspace

### 3. Member webapp

The member webapp should stop feeling like a thin utility layer and become a polished, personal product experience.

Phase 8 should make it obvious to a member:

- where they are in their journey
- what the next action is
- what their status means
- what they can unlock
- why their activity matters

The key surfaces are:

- Community Home
- Onboarding rail
- Comeback rail
- Rewards
- Notifications
- Profile
- Status and recognition surfaces

### 4. Bot surfaces

Discord and Telegram should feel product-native.

Phase 8 is not about adding entirely new bot systems. It is about polishing:

- command output
- fallback and failure copy
- deep-link language
- role and permission clarity
- consistency between Discord and Telegram

The target commands remain:

- `/link`
- `/profile`
- `/missions`
- `/leaderboard`
- `/raid`
- `/captain`

### 5. Legal and support

This is a real public launch, so legal and support cannot remain implicit.

Required launch surfaces:

- privacy policy
- terms
- support/contact route
- reward participation disclaimer where needed

The tone of these pages still needs to match the product. They should feel serious and clear, not pasted in as generic afterthoughts.

### 6. Final QA layer

This is the final cross-surface validation pass over:

- portal
- webapp
- bots
- create/edit/publish flows
- support and escalation routes
- mobile and desktop behavior

The final QA goal is to remove:

- dead ends
- hidden flows
- weak empty states
- inconsistent copy
- visual regressions
- route confusion

## Workstreams

Phase 8 is divided into seven workstreams.

### Workstream 1: Brand and messaging alignment

This workstream creates one consistent language for Veltrix.

It includes:

- founder-first public messaging
- product naming consistency
- copy cleanup across surfaces
- removal of internal or admin-sounding language where it weakens the experience

Key product language should stay consistent for:

- Project OS
- Community OS
- Member Journey
- Activation
- Trust
- Payouts
- On-chain
- Launch posture
- Support escalations

### Workstream 2: Public launch site

This workstream builds the public-facing narrative and entry path.

It includes:

- hero and positioning
- product narrative
- architecture and workflow proof
- CTA model
- FAQ
- support/legal footer

This site should sell confidence through clarity and product depth, not through inflated claims.

### Workstream 3: Portal launch polish

This workstream is a final consistency pass over the operator product.

It includes:

- hierarchy refinement
- spacing and rhythm cleanup
- action placement consistency
- empty state cleanup
- helper copy cleanup
- route clarity cleanup
- final studio quality pass

### Workstream 4: Webapp launch polish

This workstream makes the member experience feel premium and personal.

It includes:

- journey hero polish
- onboarding and comeback clarity
- status and recognition polish
- stronger reward and notification flow language
- better mobile behavior on the core member rails

### Workstream 5: Bot launch polish

This workstream makes the bot layer feel complete and product-native.

It includes:

- response copy cleanup
- better output polish
- clearer error and fallback responses
- better deep-link phrasing
- consistency between Discord and Telegram tone

### Workstream 6: Legal and support layer

This workstream adds the public legitimacy layer:

- privacy policy
- terms
- support/contact
- reward disclaimer

It should also make support escalation routes and help posture clearer for users and operators.

### Workstream 7: Final cross-surface QA

This workstream is a real acceptance sweep over the whole system.

It includes:

- portal route-by-route QA
- webapp route-by-route QA
- bot command QA
- launch/create/edit/publish/resolve flows
- desktop and mobile checks
- final polish fixes

## Scope

### In scope

- public launch site
- brand and messaging consistency
- portal final polish
- webapp final polish
- bot final polish
- legal and support pages
- final cross-surface QA

### Out of scope

Phase 8 is not for opening another major feature phase.

Explicitly out of scope:

- new major trust systems
- new major payout systems
- new major on-chain systems
- new member journey subsystems
- new community OS subsystems
- pricing engine redesign
- multi-language support
- case-study engine
- CMS platform rollout

Small quality-driven fixes are in scope when they directly serve launch polish.

New major product modules are not.

## Design principles

Phase 8 should follow these principles:

### 1. One product, not many tools

Every surface should feel like it belongs to the same brand and system.

### 2. Product proof over marketing fluff

Veltrix must earn trust through what it clearly does, not through empty claims.

### 3. Premium clarity over decorative noise

The product should feel sharp and deliberate, not overloaded.

### 4. Founders first, operators respected

The public site should speak clearly to founders first, without making growth and ops buyers feel invisible.

### 5. No hidden paths to core value

Users should not need to memorize routes or already know the system to find the main flows.

### 6. Legal and support should feel native

Public launch hygiene should feel integrated into the product, not bolted on.

## Build order

Phase 8 should be implemented in this order:

1. brand and messaging alignment
2. public launch site
3. portal polish pass
4. webapp polish pass
5. bot polish pass
6. legal and support layer
7. final cross-surface QA

This order matters because:

- the brand and messaging system defines the tone for everything that follows
- the public site needs that messaging before it can be finished cleanly
- product surfaces should then be pulled up to the same standard
- legal and support should match the finished brand posture
- QA should validate the whole system only after the surfaces are already aligned

## Exit criteria

Phase 8 is done when all of the following are true:

- the public site clearly explains Veltrix without fake social proof
- portal, webapp, and bots feel like one coherent product family
- there are no obvious hidden flows, magic routes, or embarrassing dead ends
- legal and support routes are public-launch ready
- the product can be demoed and launched without caveats
- Veltrix feels premium, serious, and proudly public across the full scope
